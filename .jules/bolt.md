## 2024-05-24 - Preserving semantics when substituting O(N) loops with O(1) dict lookups
**Learning:** In the BioNetGen codebase, biological components like molecules and structural components often do not have globally unique names within a complex (e.g. homomultimers have two of the exact same molecules in a complex). Attempting to optimize `O(N)` loop lookups using a simple `name` keyed dictionary (i.e., `dict[name] = element`) will silently drop subsequent identical elements.
**Action:** When creating a dictionary map for components/molecules by name, use a grouping structure (like `defaultdict(list)` or lists) instead of a simple mapping. Always loop over the list of matched items to ensure duplicate items receive the exact same treatment as they would in an `[x for x in components if x.name == name]` loop.
